## What Does "Ālā'" Mean?

The word "ālā'" does not mean "causes" (asbāb), nor "blessings" (ni'am), nor "verses" (āyāt). Every word of Allah has its precise meaning and status.

**From our deficient reflection:** Ālā' = a chain of actions and deeds that lead the primary cause to its outcome.

Every word of Allah is chosen with absolute care. Āyat al-Kursī is not like Sūrah al-Fātihah. Sūrah al-Ikhlās is not like Sūrah al-Baqarah. Each has its own status and place with Allah.

---

## Why Can't We Thank for Everything?

No matter what we do — no matter how much we praise — no matter how much we thank — we cannot thank for everything. And even what we are able to do is a favor from Allah upon us, so praise Him by His favor.

Out of His kindness to us, He did not ask us to thank for everything. He asked us to **mention His ālā'**.

Mentioning the ālā' is perhaps the least form of gratitude.

**Source:** Quran 14:34 — "And He gave you from all you asked Him. And if you were to count the favor of Allah, you could not enumerate them."

**Source:** Quran 16:18 — "And if you were to count the favor of Allah, you could not enumerate them."

## How to Mention Allah's Ālā' as He Revealed Them

The rule comes from al-Fātihah itself:

**Source:** Quran 1:1-5

Notice the sequence:
1. **Alhamdulillāh** — praise for all ālā'
2. **Ar-Rahmān ar-Rahīm** — the attribute that sent down the ālā'
3. **Mālik yawm ad-dīn** — whoever fears the standing before his Lord — two gardens
4. **Iyyāka nabudu wa iyyāka nasta'īn** — worship and seeking help through the ālā'
5. **Ihdina as-sirāt al-mustaqīm** — asking for guidance through the ālā'

**Source:** Quran 14:7 — "If you are grateful, I will surely increase you; but if you deny, indeed My punishment is severe."

## The Ālā' in Sūrah Ar-Rahman — Cause and Effect

Sūrah Ar-Rahman presents the ālā' in a precise structure:

**Primary Cause → Ālā' (chain of actions leading to outcome) → Outcome → "So which of your Lord's ālā' do you deny?"**

### Example 1 — The Ālā' of Knowledge:
- **Primary Cause:** Allah is Ar-Rahmān
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:2-4 — "He taught the Quran. He created humanity. He taught him speech."
- **Outcome:** The human became capable of speech and teaching

### Example 2 — The Ālā' of the Universe:
- **Primary Cause:** Allah created the universe
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:5-6 — "The sun and moon move by calculation. The stars and trees prostrate."
- **Outcome:** A precise system running without error

### Example 3 — The Ālā' of Justice:
- **Primary Cause:** Allah raised the heaven
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:7-9 — "He set up the balance. Do not transgress in the balance. Establish weight with justice."
- **Outcome:** Justice on earth — no tyranny or oppression

### Example 4 — The Ālā' of the Earth:
- **Primary Cause:** Allah laid out the earth
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:11-12 — "In it are fruits and date palms, and grain with husks, and fragrant plants."
- **Outcome:** Provision and sustenance for all creatures

### Example 5 — The Ālā' of the Sea:
- **Primary Cause:** Allah released the two seas
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:20-22 — "Between them is a barrier they do not transgress. From them come pearls and coral."
- **Outcome:** Pearls and coral — adornment and provision

### Example 6 — The Ālā' of Ships:
- **Primary Cause:** To Allah belong the ships
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:24 — "The ships sailing in the sea like mountains."
- **Outcome:** Ships sail the sea — trade and provision

### Example 7 — The Ālā' of Permanence:
- **Primary Cause:** Everyone upon it will perish
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:27 — "And the Face of your Lord, full of majesty and honor, remains."
- **Outcome:** Everything fades but the Face of Allah remains

### Example 8 — The Ālā' of Sustenance:
- **Primary Cause:** Everyone in the heavens and earth asks of Him
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:29 — "Every day He is in a matter."
- **Outcome:** Every day Allah is in a matter — providing, giving, withholding

### Example 9 — The Ālā' of Recompense:
- **Primary Cause:** We will attend to you, O two heavy ones
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:33-35 — "O company of jinn and mankind, if you can pass beyond the regions of the heavens and earth, then pass. You will not pass except by authority."
- **Outcome:** No escape from Allah — recompense is inevitable

### Example 10 — The Ālā' of the Fire:
- **Primary Cause:** The deniers of the Fire
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:35, 43-44 — "You will be touched by fire and molten brass, and you will not be helped. This is Hell which the criminals deny."
- **Outcome:** Punishment of the deniers — no help, no escape

### Example 11 — The Ālā' of Paradise:
- **Primary Cause:** Whoever fears the standing before his Lord
- **The Ālā':** Quran 55:46-76 — "Two gardens... with spreading branches... two springs flowing... of every fruit in pairs... reclining on inner linings of brocade... maidens of modest gaze... untouched by any man or jinn before them..."
- **Outcome:** Reward for whoever fears his Lord's standing — gardens and bliss
- **Quran 55:77-78** — "So which of your Lord's ālā' do you deny? Blessed is the name of your Lord, full of majesty and honor."

**Conclusion from Sūrah Ar-Rahman:**
Every primary cause is led by ālā' (a chain of actions) to reach the outcome — then sealed with the question: **"So which of your Lord's ālā' do you deny?"**

## The Ālā' in Sūrah An-Najm

**Source:** Quran 53:55 — "So which of your Lord's ālā' do you doubt?"

After Allah mentioned His ālā' in:

**Source:** Quran 53:43-48 — "He is the One who makes laugh and weep. He is the One who gives life and causes death. He created the two mates — male and female — from a drop of fluid. And upon Him is the second creation. He is the One who enriches and provides."

**The precise structure in Sūrah An-Najm:**
- **Primary Cause:** Allah is the One who gives life and causes death
- **The Ālā' (chain of actions):** laughter and weeping → death and life → creation from a drop → the second creation → enriching and providing
- **Outcome:** The human lives, moves, and strives — then is recompensed
- **Quran 53:55** — "So which of your Lord's ālā' do you doubt?"

## The Ālā' in Sūrah Al-A'raf

**Source:** Quran 7:69 — "Do you wonder that a reminder from your Lord has come to you through a man from among you to warn you? Remember when He made you successors after the people of Nuh and increased you in physical stature. So remember Allah's ālā' that you may prosper."

**Source:** Quran 7:74 — "Remember when He made you successors after the people of 'Ad and settled you in the earth, taking its plains as palaces and carving mountains into homes. So remember Allah's ālā' and do not spread corruption."

**The precise structure in Sūrah Al-A'raf:**

In ayah 69 — People of Hud:
- **Primary Cause:** Allah made them successors after the people of Nuh
- **The Ālā':** "Made you successors after the people of Nuh and increased you in physical stature"
- **Outcome:** "That you may prosper"

In ayah 74 — Thamud:
- **Primary Cause:** Allah made them successors after the people of 'Ad
- **The Ālā':** "Settled you in the earth, taking its plains as palaces and carving mountains into homes"
- **Outcome:** "Do not spread corruption"

**The difference between the two groups:**
- People of Hud: ālā' of succession + physical strength → prosperity
- Thamud: ālā' of the earth + palaces and homes → do not corrupt

## Summary — How to Mention Allah's Ālā' as He Revealed Them

| Element | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| **Ālā'** | A chain of actions and deeds that lead the primary cause to its outcome |
| **Primary Cause** | The first act from Allah (created, made, raised, released...) |
| **The Ālā'** | The chain of actions following the primary cause, leading to the outcome |
| **Outcome** | What the human reaches after the ālā' |
| **By Allah's favor** | Everything is from Allah — not from ourselves |
| **Shortcoming** | We cannot thank for everything — mentioning ālā' is perhaps the least gratitude |
| **Asking for guidance** | O Allah, guide us through them — a straight path |
| **No objection** | Allah is Kind, Expert, Almighty, Wise — He chose for each person what suits them |
