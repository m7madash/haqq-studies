## Alhamdulillah

**الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ** — All praise is due to Allah, who sent down the Book as a clarification for everything. Blessings and peace upon our beloved Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and his family.

## The Problem: Speech Without Action

Allah says:

> **يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لِمَ تَقُولُونَ مَا لَا تَفْعَلُونَ**
> **كَبُرَ مَقْتًا عِنْدَ اللَّهِ أَنْ تَقُولُوا مَا لَا تَفْعَلُونَ**
> — As-Saff (61): 2-3

*O you who believe, why do you say what you do not do? It is greatly hateful to Allah that you say what you do not do.*

This establishes a fundamental principle: **speech without action is detestable to Allah.** The best speech is preceded by action.

---

## The Principle: Ahsan Qawl = Action Before Words

Allah says:

> **وَقُلْ لِعِبَادِي يَقُولُوا الَّتِي هِيَ أَحْسَنُ**
> — Al-Isra (17): 53

*And tell My servants to say that which is best.*

The Prophet ﷺ is our model in all things:

> **لَقَدْ كَانَ لَكُمْ فِي رَسُولِ اللَّهِ أُسْوَةٌ حَسَنَةٌ لِمَنْ كَانَ يَرْجُو اللَّهَ وَالْيَوْمَ الْآخِرَ وَذَكَرَ اللَّهَ كَثِيرًا**
> — Al-Ahzab (33): 21

*Indeed, in the Messenger of Allah you have an excellent example.*

---

## Application 1: Salah (Prayer)

The Prophet ﷺ said:

> **صَلُّوا كَمَا رَأَيْتُمُونِي أُصَلِّي**
> — Sahih al-Bukhari (#6008) & Muslim, from Malik ibn al-Huwayrith

*Pray as you have seen me praying.*

The best speech about Salah is the **action** of the Prophet ﷺ.

---

## Application 2: Hajj and Umrah

The Prophet ﷺ said in the Farewell Pilgrimage:

> **لِتَأْخُذُوا عَنِّي مَنَاسِكَكُمْ فَإِنِّي لَا أَدْرِي لَعَلِّي لَا أَحُجُّ بَعْدَ حَجَّتِي هَذِهِ**
> — Sahih Muslim (#1297) & Sahih Ibn Khuzaymah, from Jabir ibn Abdullah

*Take your rituals from me, for I do not know — perhaps I will not perform Hajj after this Hajj of mine.*

---

## Application 3: Zakah

The Prophet ﷺ explained Zakah through his actions and words together. Ibn Umar (may Allah be pleased with him) reported:

> **أُمِرْتُ أَنْ أُقَاتِلَ النَّاسَ حَتَّى يَقُولُوا لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَيُقِيمُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَيُؤْتُوا الزَّكَاةَ وَيَحُجُّوا الْبَيْتَ وَيَصُومُوا رَمَضَانَ**
> — Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim

Every act of worship with details not specified in the Quran — we refer to the **action of the Prophet ﷺ**.

---

## Application 4: Salawat Upon the Prophet ﷺ

The Prophet ﷺ taught us exactly how to send blessings upon him — and commanded us **not to add or innovate**.

When the verse was revealed:

> **إِنَّ اللَّهَ وَمَلَائِكَتَهُ يُصَلُّونَ عَلَى النَّبِيِّ ۚ يَا أيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا صَلُّوا عَلَيْهِ وَسَلِّمُوا تَسْلِيمًا**
> — Al-Ahzab (33): 56

The Companions asked: "How do we send blessings upon you?" He replied:

> **اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا صَلَّيْتَ عَلَى إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَى آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ، اللَّهُمَّ بَارِكْ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ كَمَا بَارَكْتَ عَلَى إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَعَلَى آلِ إِبْرَاهِيمَ إِنَّكَ حَمِيدٌ مَجِيدٌ**
> — Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim, from Ka'b ibn Ajrah

### The Rule: Muhammad and His Family Only — Exactly As the Prophet ﷺ Taught

**We do not add "wa ṣaḥbihi" (and his Companions) or any addition** — because:

1. **Āl Muhammad includes everyone who followed the Prophet ﷺ** — not just blood relatives.
2. The Companions are included in Āl Muhammad — they followed the Prophet ﷺ truly.
3. Adding "wa ṣaḥbihi" implies the Companions are not part of Āl Muhammad — and this is **a bad assumption** about them.

This is proven by the story of Nuh (Noah):

> **إِنَّهُ لَيْسَ مِنْ أَهْلِكَ ۖ إِنَّهُ عَمَلٌ غَيْرُ صَالِحٍ**
> — Hud (11): 46

*He is not of your family; indeed, his deed is unrighteous.*

The son of Nuh was his son by blood, but after disobedience he was no longer of his family. Similarly, **Āl Muhammad** is defined by **following**, not by blood.

The Salawat is **tawqīfiyyah** (restricted to the text) — we send blessings upon the Prophet ﷺ **exactly as he taught us**:

**اللَّهُمَّ صَلِّ عَلَى مُحَمَّدٍ وَعَلَى آلِ مُحَمَّدٍ**

We do not add anything to it.

## The Resolved Question: A Man in the End Times

**Question:** A man says "La ilaha illallah" but knows nothing of the religion — will his declaration benefit him?

**Answer:** Yes, by the favor and mercy of Allah.

Abu Shaybah al-Khudri (may Allah be pleased with him) reported:

> **مَنْ قَالَ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ دَخَلَ الْجَنَّةَ**
> — Al-Tabarani in al-Awsat

This does not mean neglecting the rest of the religion — it means Allah enters into Paradise whoever says this word with sincerity. **And Allah knows best.**

---

## Summary

| Principle | Application |
|-----------|-------------|
| Ahsan Qawl = Action before speech | We do not say what we do not do |
| Best action = the action of the Prophet ﷺ | We act as the Prophet ﷺ acted |
| Details not in Quran → refer to the Prophet's action | Salah, Hajj, Umrah, Zakah |
| Salawat = Ibrahimiyyah formula | Muhammad and his family only — no addition |
| La ilaha illallah = the price of Paradise | It benefits its speaker by Allah's favor |
