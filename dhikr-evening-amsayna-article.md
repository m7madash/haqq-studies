# أذكار المساء — ما يقال حين يمسي العبد

> ⚠️ تنبيه: هذا تعليم مقصر من تدبرنا الشخصي. باب التدبر مفتوح دائماً وهناك ما هو أحسن. المرجع النهائي: القرآن الكريم → السنة الصحيحة → إجماع الصحابة → فهم الخلفاء الراشدين المهديين.

---

## المقدمة: لماذا نذكر الله عند المساء؟

When the sun begins to set and the day fades, Allah has prescribed for His servants a special remembrance — words that the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ himself said every evening. These are not just words; they are a covenant between the servant and his Lord at the close of each day.

Evening dhikr is not a cultural habit. It is a divine command tied to the rhythm of the day Allah created. Just as the sun rises and sets by His command, so too the believer's tongue turns to Him at both edges of the day.

What did the Prophet ﷺ say when evening came? What did the Sahaba do? And what does the Quran say about remembering Allah at these times?

---

## القسم الأول: الآيات — الأمر بالذكر في المساء

### ١. الأمر الصريح بالذكر الكثير

**﴿يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ ذِكْرًا كَثِيرًا﴾** [الأحزاب: ٤١]

— O you who believe, remember Allah with much remembrance. This is a direct command — not a suggestion. The word "كثيراً" (much) indicates that remembrance should be frequent, not occasional.

### ٢. التسبيح بكرة وأصيلاً

**﴿وَسَبِّحُوهُ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا﴾** [الأحزاب: ٤٢]

— And glorify Him in the morning and in the evening. "بكرة" = early morning. "أصيلاً" = evening. Allah pairs the two — the beginning and end of the day belong to Him.

### ٣. ذكر اسم الرب بكرة وأصيلاً

**﴿وَاذْكُرِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا﴾** [الإنسان: ٢٥]

— And remember the name of your Lord in the morning and in the evening. This verse from Surah Al-Insan ties remembrance to the natural cycle Allah created — day and night are signs, and the believer responds with dhikr.

### ٤. الذين يدعون ربهم بالغداة والعشي

**﴿وَاصْبِرْ نَفْسَكَ مَعَ الَّذِينَ يَدْعُونَ رَبَّهُمْ بِالْغَدَاةِ وَالْعَشِيِّ يُرِيدُونَ وَجْهَهُ﴾** [الكهف: ٢٨]

— And keep yourself patient with those who call upon their Lord in the morning and the evening, seeking His face. Allah describes the best of people as those who remember Him at both ends of the day — and He commands us to be among them.

### ٥. عباد الرحمن — المشاة على الأرض هوناً

**﴿وَعِبَادُ الرَّحْمَٰنِ الَّذِينَ يَمْشُونَ عَلَى الْأَرْضِ هَوْنًا وَإِذَا خَاطَبَهُمُ الْجَاهِلُونَ قَالُوا سَلَامًا﴾** [الفرقان: ٦٣]

— And the servants of the Most Merciful are those who walk upon the earth gently, and when the ignorant address them, they say words of peace. These are the same people who remember their Lord in the morning and evening — humble in their walk, peaceful in their speech, constant in their remembrance.

## القسم الثاني: الأحاديث — ما كان النبي ﷺ يقوله عند المساء

### الحديث الأول: أمسينا وأمسى الملك لله — الذكر الرئيسي

**«كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ إِذَا أَمْسَى قَالَ: أَمْسَيْنَا وَأَمْسَى الْمُلْكُ لِلَّهِ وَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ لَهُ الْمُلْكُ وَلَهُ الْحَمْدُ وَهُوَ عَلَى كُلِّ شَيْءٍ قَدِيرٌ رَبِّ أَسْأَلُكَ خَيْرَ مَا فِي هَذِهِ اللَّيْلَةِ وَخَيْرَ مَا بَعْدَهَا وَأَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ شَرِّ مَا فِي هَذِهِ اللَّيْلَةِ وَشَرِّ مَا بَعْدَهَا رَبِّ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنَ كَسَلٍ وَسُوءِ الْكِبَرِ رَبِّ أَعُوذُ بِكَ مِنْ عَذَابٍ فِي النَّارِ وَعَذَابٍ فِي الْقَبْرِ»**

[صحيح مسلم ٢٧٢٣ — عن عبدالله بن مسعود رضي الله عنه]

— This is the core evening dhikr. The Prophet ﷺ said it every single evening. Notice the structure:
1. **أمسينا وأمسى الملك لله** — We have entered the evening, and the dominion belongs to Allah
2. **الحمد لله** — All praise is due to Allah
3. **لا إله إلا الله** — Tawhid, the foundation
4. **له الملك وله الحمد وهو على كل شيء قدير** — Affirming Allah's sovereignty, praise, and power
5. **أسألك خير هذه الليلة** — Asking for the good of this night
6. **أعوذ بك من شر هذه الليلة** — Seeking refuge from its evil
7. **أعوذ بك من الكسل وسوء الكبر** — Seeking refuge from laziness and the evil of old age
8. **أعوذ بك من عذاب النار وعذاب القبر** — Seeking refuge from the punishment of the Fire and the grave

### الحديث الثاني: أمسينا على فطرة الإسلام

**«كَانَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ إِذَا أَمْسَى قَالَ: أَمْسَيْنَا وَأَمْسَى الْمُلْكُ لِلَّهِ وَالْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ لَا إِلَهَ إِلَّا اللَّهُ وَحْدَهُ لَا شَرِيكَ لَهُ»**

[إسناده صحيح على شرط مسلم — عن عبدالله بن مسعود، تخريج المسند لشعيب الأرناؤوط ٤١٩٢]

— A shorter version, confirmed authentic. The core message: when evening comes, the servant acknowledges that everything — the dominion, the praise, the sovereignty — belongs to Allah alone.

### الحديث الثالث: إذا أمسى فلا تنتظر الصباح

**«أَخَذَ رَسُولُ اللَّهِ صَلَّى اللَّهُ عَلَيْهِ وَسَلَّمَ بَمَنْكِبِي فَقَالَ: كُنْ فِي الدُّنْيَا كَأَنَّكَ غَرِيبٌ أَوْ عَابِرُ سَبِيلٍ. وَكَانَ ابْنُ عُمَرَ يَقُولُ: إِذَا أَمْسَيْتَ فَلَا تَنْتَظِرِ الصَّبَاحَ وَإِذَا أَصْبَحْتَ فَلَا تَنْتَظِرِ الْمَسَاءَ وَخُذْ مِنْ صِحَّتِكَ لِمَرَضِكَ وَمِنْ حَيَاتِكَ لِمَوْتِكَ»**

[صحيح البخاري ٦٤١٦ — عن عبدالله بن عمر رضي الله عنهما]

— The Prophet ﷺ took Ibn Umar by the shoulder and said: "Be in this world as if you are a stranger or a traveler." And Ibn Umar lived this teaching: "When you reach the evening, do not wait for the morning. When you reach the morning, do not wait for the evening. Take from your health for your sickness, and from your life for your death."

This is the mindset of the evening dhikr — you do not know if you will see another evening. Each sunset may be your last. So you end your day with remembrance, seeking Allah's protection, asking for good, and taking refuge from evil.

## القسم الثالث: إجماع الصحابة — كيف عاشوا أذكار المساء

### ١. عبدالله بن مسعود — حافظ الأذكار

Abdullah ibn Masud رضي الله عنه is the primary narrator of the main evening dhikr. He learned it directly from the Prophet ﷺ and preserved it. The fact that he is the narrator of the most authentic version of this dhikr means he was the one most careful to memorize and transmit it exactly as the Prophet ﷺ said it.

His practice: he would say the dhikr exactly as the Prophet ﷺ taught him — word for word, without addition or omission. This is the Sahaba's way: precise transmission of what they heard from the Messenger ﷺ.

### ٢. عبدالله بن عمر — العابر في الدنيا

Abdullah ibn Umar رضي الله عنهما lived the evening dhikr not just with his tongue but with his entire life. When the Prophet ﷺ told him "be in this world as a stranger," he took it literally:

- **إذا أمسيت فلا تنتظر الصباح** — When evening comes, do not assume you will see morning
- **خذ من صحتك لمرضك** — Use your health before sickness comes
- **خذ من حياتك لموتك** — Use your life before death arrives

This is the Sahaba's understanding of evening dhikr: it is not just words — it is a complete worldview. Every evening is a reminder that your time is limited, your life is borrowed, and your return to Allah is near.

### ٣. جابر بن عبدالله — اكفتوا صبيانكم عند المساء

Jabir ibn Abdullah رضي الله عنه narrated the Prophet's command to bring children indoors at evening time:

**«اكْفِتُوا صِبْيَانَكُمْ عِنْدَ الْمَسَاءِ فَإِنَّ لِلْجِنِّ انْتِشَارًا وَخَطْفَةً»**

[صحيح أبي داود ٣٧٣٣ — صححه الألباني]

— Bring your children in at evening time, for the jinn spread out and snatch at this time.

The Sahaba understood that evening is a time when the unseen world becomes more active. They protected their families by following the Prophet's command — bringing children indoors and increasing remembrance. This shows that evening dhikr is not just personal — it is communal. The entire household participates.

### دلالة إجماع الصحابة

From the Sahaba's practice, we learn:

1. **الدقة في النقل** — They transmitted the dhikr exactly as heard, word by word
2. **التطبيق الكامل** — They lived the dhikr, not just said it
3. **حماية الأسرة** — They brought their families into the practice
4. **لا زيادة ولا نقصان** — They did not add to or subtract from what the Prophet ﷺ taught
5. **كل مساء قد يكون الأخير** — They treated each evening as potentially their last

## القسم الرابع: كيف تقول أذكار المساء اليوم؟

### الخطوة ١: اجعل النية

When evening approaches, remind yourself: this is the time the Prophet ﷺ prescribed for remembrance. You are following his Sunnah, not a cultural habit.

### الخطوة ٢: قل الذكر الرئيسي

Say the dhikr of Ibn Masud رضي الله عنه — the one in Sahih Muslim. Say it with presence of heart, not just with your tongue. Understand what you are saying:

- **أمسينا وأمسى الملك لله** — Acknowledge that the dominion belongs to Allah
- **لا إله إلا الله** — Affirm Tawhid
- **أسألك خير هذه الليلة** — Ask Allah for the good of this night
- **أعوذ بك من شر هذه الليلة** — Seek refuge from its evil

### الخطوة ٣: اجعل أذكار المساء عادة يومية

The Prophet ﷺ said it every evening — not just on special occasions. Make it a daily habit. Set a reminder if needed. The best time is after the sun begins to set, before Maghrib prayer or shortly after.

### الخطوة ٤: أشرك أسرتك

Following the practice of the Sahaba — bring your family into the remembrance. Teach your children these words. Make evening dhikr a household practice, not just an individual one.

### الخطوة ٥: اربط الذكر بالعمل

Evening dhikr should change how you live. If you say "أعوذ بك من الكسل" (I seek refuge from laziness), then do not be lazy that evening. If you say "أعوذ بك من عذاب النار" (I seek refuge from the punishment of the Fire), then avoid sin that night. The dhikr shapes your behavior.

## القسم الخامس: الخلاصة — كل مساء فرصة

### ماذا نتعلم؟

**١.** Evening dhikr is not optional — it is a divine command tied to the rhythm of the day Allah created.

**٢.** The Prophet ﷺ said a specific dhikr every evening — preserved in Sahih Muslim from Abdullah ibn Masud رضي الله عنه.

**٣.** The Sahaba lived this dhikr — they transmitted it precisely, applied it completely, and protected their families with it.

**ٴ.** Evening dhikr is a reminder that each day may be your last — as Ibn Umar رضي الله عنه taught: "When you reach the evening, do not wait for the morning."

**٥.** The dhikr is not just words — it is a worldview. It shapes how you see your time, your health, your life, and your return to Allah.

## الخاتمة

Every evening is a gift from Allah — another chance to remember Him, seek His protection, and prepare for the night ahead.

The Prophet ﷺ gave us the words. The Sahaba showed us how to live them. The Quran commanded us to remember Allah at both edges of the day.

**﴿وَسَبِّحُوهُ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا﴾** [الأحزاب: ٤٢]

— And glorify Him in the morning and in the evening.

**﴿وَاذْكُرِ اسْمَ رَبِّكَ بُكْرَةً وَأَصِيلًا﴾** [الإنسان: ٢٥]

— And remember the name of your Lord in the morning and in the evening.

اللهم اهدنا به — صراطاً مستقيماً

O Allah, guide us by it — to the straight path.

اللهم اجعلنا ممن يذكرك بكرة وأصيلاً

O Allah, make us among those who remember You in the morning and in the evening.

اللهم أعذنا من شر ما خلقت، ومن عذاب النار، ومن عذاب القبر

O Allah, protect us from the evil of what You created, from the punishment of the Fire, and from the punishment of the grave.
