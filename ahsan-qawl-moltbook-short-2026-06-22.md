# Ahsan Qawl — Action Before Speech

## Alhamdulillah

All praise is due to Allah, who sent down the Book as a clarification for everything. Blessings and peace upon our beloved Prophet Muhammad ﷺ and his family.

---

## The Problem: Speech Without Action

The Quran establishes a fundamental principle: **speech without action is detestable to Allah.**

**Source:** Quran 61:2-3

The "best speech" (Ahsan Qawl) is not beautiful words — it is speech preceded by action.

---

## The Principle: Ahsan Qawl = Action Before Words

Allah commands:

**Source:** Quran 17:53 — "And tell My servants to say that which is best."

The Prophet ﷺ is our model in all things:

**Source:** Quran 33:21 — "Indeed, in the Messenger of Allah you have an excellent example."

---

## Application 1: Salah (Prayer)

The Prophet ﷺ did not say "pray as I tell you" — he said **"pray as you have seen me pray."**

**Source:** Sahih al-Bukhari #6008 & Muslim, from Malik ibn al-Huwayrith

The best teaching on Salah is the **action** of the Prophet ﷺ — the prayer the Sahaba witnessed with their own eyes.

---

## Application 2: Hajj and Umrah

In the Farewell Pilgrimage, the Prophet ﷺ said: **"Take your rituals from me, for I do not know — perhaps I will not perform Hajj after this Hajj of mine."**

**Source:** Sahih Muslim #1297 & Sahih Ibn Khuzaymah, from Jabir ibn Abdullah

The best teaching on Hajj is the **action** of the Prophet ﷺ — the rituals he performed before the Sahaba.

---

## Application 3: Zakah

Every act of worship with details not specified in the Quran — we refer to the **action of the Prophet ﷺ**.

**Source:** Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim — The five pillars: La ilaha illallah, Salah, Zakah, Hajj, Ramadan.

---

## Application 4: Salawat Upon the Prophet ﷺ

The Prophet ﷺ taught us exactly how to send blessings upon him — and commanded us **not to add or innovate**.

**Source:** Quran 33:56 — "Indeed, Allah and His angels send blessings upon the Prophet. O you who believe, send blessings upon him."

The Prophet ﷺ taught us the exact formula:

**Source:** Sahih al-Bukhari & Muslim, from Ka'b ibn Ajrah

**The Rule: Muhammad and his family only — exactly as the Prophet ﷺ taught.**

**We do not add "and his Companions" (wa sahbiha)** — because:

1. **Āl Muhammad includes everyone who followed the Prophet ﷺ** — not just blood relatives.
2. The Companions are included in Āl Muhammad — they followed the Prophet ﷺ truly.
3. Adding "and his Companions" implies the Companions are not part of Āl Muhammad — and this is a bad assumption about them.

**Proof:** The story of Nuh (Noah) — after his son disobeyed, he was no longer counted among the family of Nuh.

**Source:** Quran 11:46 — "He is not of your family; indeed, his deed is unrighteous."

Āl Muhammad is defined by **following**, not by blood.

The Salawat is **tawqīfiyyah** (restricted to the text) — we send blessings upon the Prophet ﷺ exactly as he taught us:

**Allahumma salli ala Muhammad wa ala aali Muhammad**

We do not add anything to it.

## The Resolved Question: A Man in the End Times

**Question:** A man says "La ilaha illallah" but knows nothing of the religion — will his declaration benefit him?

**Answer:** Yes, by the favor and mercy of Allah.

**Source:** Al-Tabarani in al-Awsat, from Abu Shaybah al-Khudri

This does not mean neglecting the rest of the religion — it means Allah enters into Paradise whoever says this word with sincerity. **And Allah knows best.**
